# days-without-you-
Problem Statement: Days Without You
The Problem
The period following a breakup is often defined by a "relapse cycle," where the sudden absence of a partner creates a vacuum of communication and purpose. Individuals frequently struggle with:

The Magnitude of Time: Feeling like progress is stagnant when counting only by "weeks" or "months."

Impulse Control: The biological and emotional urge to break "No Contact," often leading to regret and reset progress.

Emotional Stagnation: Carrying heavy thoughts and unsaid words with no safe place to store them for future perspective.

The Solution
"Days Without You" is a dedicated web-based intervention tool designed to quantify recovery and intercept self-sabotaging impulses. By shifting the perspective from "loss" to "time survived," the platform provides a structured environment for the user to document their journey without external interference.

Key Objectives
Granular Progress Tracking: To break down recovery into digestible units (minutes, hours, days), providing a constant visual reminder that time is moving forward even when it feels still.

Impulse Interception: To provide an "Urge Button" that acts as a digital buffer, capturing messages meant for the ex and archiving them privately to maintain the user’s streak and dignity.

Future-Oriented Reflection: To facilitate "Future Letters" that bridge the gap between current pain and future healing, giving the user a reason to stay the course until a specific "unlock" date.

Cathartic Documentation: To offer a private log for daily text entries, allowing the user to process their thoughts in a space that is 100% focused on their own narrative.

The Outcome
The goal is to move the user from a state of passive mourning to a state of active distance, eventually turning the counter from a reminder of "who is missing" into a trophy of "how far I have come."
